Difference Between Claude and ChatGPT Prompt Structure

Both Claude and ChatGPT are advanced AI assistants, but they often respond best to slightly different prompt styles.

ChatGPT generally performs well when prompts are organized into clear sections with specific instructions. A common structure is:

Role: You are a marketing expert.

Task: Create a social media campaign.

Requirements:
- Target college students
- Budget under $5,000
- Duration: 30 days

Output Format:
- Weekly activity table
- Expected results

This approach helps ChatGPT follow instructions accurately because the role, task, constraints, and output format are clearly separated.

Claude, on the other hand, often works effectively with more natural and context-rich instructions. For example:

You are helping a startup reach college students through social media.

Create a 30-day campaign with a budget below $5,000. Focus on practical and cost-effective strategies. Explain the reasoning behind your recommendations and present the final plan in a weekly table.

Claude tends to use the broader context provided in the prompt and may require less rigid formatting than ChatGPT.

Key Differences
Feature	ChatGPT	Claude
Preferred Prompt Style	Structured and explicit	Natural and context-rich
Instruction Following	Highly precise	Context-driven
Formatting Requirements	Often benefits from clear sections	More flexible
Long Context Handling	Strong	Very strong
Best Practice	Role + Task + Constraints + Output Format	Context + Goal + Detailed Guidance
Conclusion

The main difference is that ChatGPT usually performs best with structured prompts, while Claude often excels with detailed, conversational instructions and rich context. However, both models can handle either style effectively when the prompt is clear and specific.
